# Corporate Contributor License Agreement (CCLA)

Version 1.0 — Effective 2026-04-20
Copyright (c) 2026 Woodfine Capital Projects Inc. All rights reserved.

## Preamble

This Corporate Contributor License Agreement ("CCLA") is entered into
between Woodfine Capital Projects Inc. ("Woodfine") and the corporation
identified in the signature block below (the "Corporation"). This CCLA
clarifies the terms under which intellectual-property Contributions made
by the Corporation's employees to Woodfine-managed repositories are
licensed to Woodfine.

This CCLA is adapted from the Apache Software Foundation Corporate
Contributor License Agreement. Where this CCLA diverges from the Apache
CCLA, the divergences are stated inline.

Execution of this CCLA means that the Corporation covers, as a single
written agreement, the Contributions of all its employees who submit
work to Woodfine-managed repositories in the course of their employment.
Individual employees so covered are NOT required to separately execute
the Individual Contributor License Agreement.

## 1. Definitions

**"Corporation"** means the entity identified in the signature block,
together with all other entities that control, are controlled by, or
are under common control with that entity. For purposes of this
definition, "control" means (i) the power, direct or indirect, to
cause the direction or management of such entity, whether by contract
or otherwise, or (ii) ownership of fifty percent (50%) or more of the
outstanding shares, or (iii) beneficial ownership of such entity.

**"Covered Employee"** means any individual employed by, or acting as a
contractor or agent of, the Corporation who submits a Contribution to a
Woodfine Repository in the course of such employment, contractor
engagement, or agency, except an individual whom the Corporation has
excluded by written notice under Section 5.

**"Contribution"** means any original work of authorship, including any
modifications or additions to an existing work, that is intentionally
submitted by a Covered Employee to Woodfine for inclusion in, or
documentation of, any Woodfine-managed repository.

**"Submit"** means any form of electronic, verbal, or written
communication sent to Woodfine or its representatives, including but
not limited to pull requests, issue comments, code review comments, and
any communication on platforms such as GitHub or mailing lists managed
by Woodfine. "Submitted" is excluded if conspicuously marked or
otherwise designated in writing as "Not a Contribution."

**"Repository"** means any Woodfine-managed repository identified in
`mapping/repo-license-map.yaml`.

## 2. Grant of Copyright License

Subject to the terms and conditions of this CCLA, the Corporation
hereby grants to Woodfine and to recipients of software distributed by
Woodfine a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, no-charge, royalty-free,
**sublicensable** copyright license to reproduce, prepare derivative
works of, publicly display, publicly perform, sublicense, and distribute
all Contributions made by Covered Employees, and derivative works
thereof, under any license terms that Woodfine elects, including but
not limited to:

  (a) the license applied to the Repository at the time of the
      Contribution (as identified in `mapping/repo-license-map.yaml`);
  (b) any subsequent license Woodfine adopts for that Repository; and
  (c) the PointSav Commercial License, which Woodfine offers as an
      alternative to AGPLv3 and FSL-1.1 for customers who cannot
      accept those terms.

## 3. Grant of Patent License

Subject to the terms and conditions of this CCLA, the Corporation
hereby grants to Woodfine and to recipients of software distributed
by Woodfine a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, no-charge,
royalty-free, irrevocable (except as stated in this section) patent
license to make, have made, use, offer to sell, sell, import, and
otherwise transfer Contributions of Covered Employees. This license
applies only to those patent claims licensable by the Corporation that
are necessarily infringed by a Contribution alone or by combination of
the Contribution with the Repository to which the Contribution was
submitted.

If any entity institutes patent litigation against the Corporation or
any other entity (including a cross-claim or counterclaim in a lawsuit)
alleging that a Contribution, or the Repository to which a Contribution
has been submitted, constitutes direct or contributory patent
infringement, then any patent licenses granted to that entity under
this CCLA for that Contribution or Repository shall terminate as of the
date such litigation is filed.

## 4. Representations

The Corporation represents that:

  (a) **The signatory has the authority** to bind the Corporation to
      this CCLA.

  (b) **The Corporation is legally entitled** to grant the above
      licenses with respect to Contributions made by its Covered
      Employees.

  (c) **Each Contribution is the original creation** of the Covered
      Employee who submitted it (see Section 7 for submissions that
      are not original work).

  (d) Contribution submissions made by Covered Employees include
      **complete details of any third-party license or other
      restriction** (including, but not limited to, related patents
      and trademarks) of which the Corporation is personally aware
      and which is associated with any part of the Contribution.

## 5. Employee Coverage and Exclusions

By default, all individuals who are, or become, employed by, engaged
as contractors by, or acting as agents of the Corporation are Covered
Employees for purposes of this CCLA. The Corporation need not notify
Woodfine as new individuals join the Corporation; they become Covered
Employees automatically.

The Corporation may exclude specific individuals from Covered Employee
status by sending written notice to Woodfine at
corporate.secretary@woodfinegroup.com. The notice shall identify the
individual by name, email address, GitHub username, and effective date
of exclusion.

An excluded individual who wishes to contribute to a Woodfine
Repository must execute the Individual Contributor License Agreement
(`individual-cla.md`) separately. An excluded individual's
Contributions are not covered by this CCLA, even if made while the
individual is employed by the Corporation.

## 6. Successors and Assigns

This CCLA binds the Corporation and its successors and assigns,
including any entity that acquires the Corporation through merger,
asset sale, reorganization, or similar transaction.

If a Covered Employee ceases to be employed by, engaged by, or an
agent of the Corporation, that individual is no longer a Covered
Employee as of the date of cessation, and this CCLA does not cover
Contributions made by that individual after that date.

## 7. No Obligation

The Corporation understands that the decision to include any
Contribution in any Repository is entirely Woodfine's and that this
CCLA does not obligate Woodfine to use, distribute, or otherwise
exploit any Contribution.

## 8. Support

Covered Employees are not expected to provide support for their
Contributions except to the extent they desire to provide support.
Contributions are provided on an "AS IS" basis, without warranties or
conditions of any kind, either express or implied, except as
expressly stated in this CCLA.

## 9. Submission of Non-Original Work

Should a Covered Employee wish to submit work that is not the Covered
Employee's original creation, the Covered Employee may submit it to
Woodfine separately from any Contribution, identifying the complete
details of its source and of any license or other restriction
(including, but not limited to, related patents, trademarks, and
license agreements) of which the Covered Employee or the Corporation
is personally aware, and conspicuously marking the work as "Submitted
on behalf of a third-party: [named here]".

## 10. Notification of Changes

The Corporation agrees to notify Woodfine of any facts or
circumstances of which the Corporation becomes aware that would make
the representations in Section 4 inaccurate in any respect.

## 11. Governing Law

This CCLA shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the
laws of the Province of Ontario and the federal laws of Canada
applicable therein, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles.

## 12. Entire Agreement

This CCLA is the entire agreement between the Corporation and
Woodfine with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all
prior and contemporaneous oral or written agreements between the
parties with respect to Contributions made by Covered Employees.

## 13. Execution

This CCLA is executed by electronic signature via a qualified
e-signature platform (DocuSign, Adobe Sign, or equivalent). The
platform must record signatory identity, IP address, timestamp, and
produce a tamper-evident document. Wet-signature execution on a
printed copy, returned to Woodfine by email or postal mail, is also
accepted.

Execution does NOT rely on CLA Assistant click-through, because the
signatory must have verifiable authority to bind the Corporation — a
click on a pull-request bot does not demonstrate that authority.

Once executed, the Corporation shall email the signed CCLA to
corporate.secretary@woodfinegroup.com. Woodfine will acknowledge
receipt and identify the CCLA in its records by a reference number.
